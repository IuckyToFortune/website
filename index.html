About

Precision at Scale

Manufacturing Engineer skilled at understanding, optimizing, and sustaining operations, processes, and tooling across high-volume semiconductor environments. At Intel, I led daily process improvements that reduced variability, improved product quality, and drove yield gains on critical process nodes.

My background spans robotics & automation projects, equipment qualification, chemical inventory management, and module quality ownership — including root cause analysis, CAPA, and technician training.

Prior to Intel, I conducted novel materials research at the University of Idaho — characterizing composites via thermal, rheological, and electrochemical techniques, and co-designing a custom pyrolysis reactor for polymer waste upcycling research.

I'm guided by the belief that great engineering is both rigorous and inventive — measurable improvements are earned by understanding systems deeply, then redesigning them boldly.
